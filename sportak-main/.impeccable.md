## Design Context

### Users
Internal Lundegaard employees competing in company sports challenges. They check the leaderboard and dashboard on **desktop** (at the office or WFH), but **record activities on mobile** after a workout. This dual context means: the dashboard should reward at-a-glance scanning on a wide screen, while the activity logging flow must be thumb-friendly, fast, and forgiving on small screens.

### Brand Personality
**Fun, playful, friendly** — this is a voluntary company initiative, not a compliance tool. It should feel like something people *want* to open, not something they have to use.

Three words: **energetic · warm · motivating**

Emotional goal: when a user opens the app, they should feel a small hit of competitive excitement — "I want to beat that person above me" — mixed with encouragement that their effort is seen and celebrated.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Reference: Fitbit app** — energetic data cards, bold numbers, progress rings/bars, friendly empty states with encouragement, clear rank display. The feel is sporty-but-approachable, not clinical or corporate.

**Anti-reference**: avoid sterile enterprise intranet aesthetics (gray tables, dense forms, no personality). Also avoid over-gamified "badge soup" — keep it clean and focused.

**Direction summary**: white cards on a light grey canvas, generous use of the brand gradient for hero moments and progress indicators, bold numerics for scores and rankings, emoji used sparingly but intentionally (medals, sport icons), subtle hover animations that feel responsive.

### Typography

Two fonts are in use — this is intentional:
- **Barlow Condensed** (`--font-display`, `font-display` Tailwind class) — scores, headings, numeric display
- **Plus Jakarta Sans** (`--font`, `font-sans` Tailwind class) — body copy, labels, form elements, microcopy

Both are loaded in `pages/_document.tsx` via a single Google Fonts link. The `impeccable` CLI scanner incorrectly flags this as `single-font` because it only reads the text content of `_document.tsx` and misses Plus Jakarta Sans in the URL string. This is a **known false positive** — do not act on it.

### Design Principles

1. **Celebrate effort visibly** — scores, ranks, and progress should be the biggest things on screen. Numbers deserve large type and strong contrast.

2. **Mobile-first for logging, desktop-first for browsing** — the activity form should be a single thumb-zone interaction. The leaderboard and challenge cards can use wider layouts.

3. **Use the brand gradient purposefully** — reserve `#18C872 → #006DFF` for elements that deserve attention: top-rank highlights, active progress bars, CTAs, the logo mark. Don't spread it everywhere.

4. **Friendly over formal** — prefer conversational Czech microcopy, rounded corners, and warm empty states over table-heavy, form-heavy layouts. This is a sports app, not an HR system.

5. **Fast feedback** — loading skeletons, optimistic UI, and clear success states so users on mobile feel the app responding immediately after logging a run.
